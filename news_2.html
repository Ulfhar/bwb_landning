DevLog #1: Achievements and Lore

Reminder: you’re reading a DevLog — thoughts and reflections from the developer of Far-Far Forest. No AI or GMO — just hand-made.

Your game — your achievements. Has a nice ring to it, doesn’t it?

Looking back, the decision to create my own achievement system was pretty spontaneous.

For some reason, I just didn’t like the default Play Market achievements — too monotonous, too boring.

Besides, it seemed like a fairly simple task I could complete quickly to take a short break from level design and focus on something else.

And for once, my estimate turned out to be spot on (that doesn’t happen often, does it?).

So what do we have now?

First of all, my own flexible system with two types of achievements: ones that you need to accumulate to unlock, and ones that are granted immediately.

The pop-ups turned out quite charming, and the overall design fits perfectly with the game’s atmosphere.

I also had an idea: what if, when you unlocked an achievement, you’d get a little piece of the world’s lore?

Because believe me — there’s so much to tell!

But I couldn’t find a convenient or intuitive way to pull it off.

I thought about adding it to the description, but since you can read that even without unlocking the achievement, the idea fell apart.

A few words about the lore — and the pain that comes with it.

I don’t know how to share it with you!

Or rather, I don’t know how to do it the way I want.

Because this text is for the “chosen ones,” those who are truly interested — so I’ll reveal a few cards (God, how that word sounds so filthy in the context of recent events).

In the Far-Far Forest, full of wonders and magic, there once lived a King — the Wise Oak.

His rule was honest and just, yet, as it often goes, not everyone shared his virtues. Even in a fairy tale.

Deep in the woods, beneath the mountain, grew the one whose cunning and bitterness brought an end to the ancient order.

There is no more Oak — Chestnut now reigns. And those who could still stand against his evil will are no more.

Little fireflies, who once gifted their light and strength to the Wise Ruler, now hide from the tyrant’s minions — the forest shadows, echoes of a life long past. They’re snatched into ancient lanterns and thrown without will under the roots of the Father of Thorny Fruits — the Great Chestnut.

How long will this silent oppression of Evil Will last? Yes, far too long.
Will it last forever? No, for a new Light has already been born that will overcome the Darkness.

This is just a fragment of the story that’s bursting to be told, and I have to find a way to share it with you.

I don’t want to shove it in your face, but I don’t want to hide it away completely either.

Anyway, this was supposed to be a text about achievements, and I got a little carried away.

But what else is there to say?

They’re here.

I made them the way I saw fit — and that’s already something.

Now I’m waiting for your feedback.
